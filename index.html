Solutions

Key to clean future

Bioethanol Production

Bioethanol is a renewable fuel derived from biomass through hydrolysis and fermentation. Our systems convert agricultural feedstock into high-purity bioethanol for domestic use and global export, supporting cleaner energy alternatives.

Biomass Conversion

We utilize locally sourced agricultural waste and energy crops to produce sustainable fuel. This approach reduces environmental waste while creating economic value across Nigeria's agricultural supply chain.

Green Fuel Export

Our purification and quality control processes ensure export-grade bioethanol that meets international standards. This enables Nigeria to participate competitively in the global renewable energy market.

Sustainable Industrial Processing

By optimizing fermentation efficiency and reducing energy loss during purification, our facilities lower emissions and improve overall production sustainability, advancing cleaner industrial energy practices.

About us

Nigeria is the world's largest producer of cassava, yet this dominance has not translated into industrial leadership, energy security, or climate finance gains. One of the most compelling pathways to unlock cassava's latent value is ethanol production. Cassava's high sink strength—its exceptional ability to convert atmospheric carbon captured in its leaves into starch stored in its roots—makes it a near-ideal bioenergy feedstock. However, Nigeria has barely scratched the surface of this opportunity, especially when viewed through the lens of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG)–driven development and financing. For example, Nigeria produces about 50m metric tons of cassava. It imports more than 90% of its bioethanol demands. KoreX has been established to bridge the gap between the Nigeria's potential and its current position
